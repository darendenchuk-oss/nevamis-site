# AI Growth System (Website Conversion and Growth System)

- Pillar: Grow
- Status: planned
- Priority: 13
- Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

The AI Growth System combines conversion-focused web experiences, lead capture, approved follow-up, customer reactivation, reviews, and revenue tracking into one connected growth system. It is the capstone of the Nevamis stack: it assumes the receptionist, follow-up, tracking, and attribution services already work, and it manages the whole acquisition journey against a single question: did the program produce more collected revenue than it cost? The brief keeps this service in the Future stage, and it is built last (Wave 4) because performance-style growth work requires reliable attribution and operating history first.

## 1. Customer problem

- The business's marketing is a pile of disconnected pieces: a website that does not convert, ads nobody measures, forms that go to an unwatched inbox, and no follow-up. Each piece may work a little, but nothing connects.
- The website is a brochure, not a conversion tool. Visitors arrive from ads or search and leave without calling, filling a form, or booking.
- The owner has hired separate vendors for the website, ads, and SEO. Each vendor reports its own vanity metric (clicks, impressions, rankings) and none of them can show collected revenue.
- Money spent on acquisition cannot be compared to money collected, so the owner cannot tell whether growth spending is an investment or a leak.
- Winning changes never compound. Nobody tests the website, nobody reuses what worked in one campaign for the next one, and past customers are never brought back.

## 2. Ideal customer profile

- Owner-operated trades and local service businesses in the Edmonton area (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, roofing, landscaping, auto repair, and similar).
- 5 to 25 staff, with capacity to take on more work and an owner who wants growth, not just coverage.
- Already an established Nevamis client running the AI Front Desk plus at least follow-up and lead tracking, so the capture and measurement layers exist. This service is not sold standalone or to new clients.
- Has an approved marketing budget and is already spending, or ready to spend, on at least one paid channel.
- Willing to approve claims, offers, budgets, and messaging before anything runs, and to share job and payment data so results can be verified.

## 3. Jobs to be done

- When someone visits my website from an ad or a search, turn that visit into a call, a form fill, or a booking instead of a bounce.
- When I spend money on marketing, connect every dollar of spend to the leads, jobs, and collected revenue it produced, so I can decide where the next dollar goes.
- When a campaign runs, make sure every lead it generates gets an instant, approved response and follow-up until the customer decides, so paid leads are not wasted.
- When something on the website or in a campaign works, keep it and build on it; when it does not, stop it, so my marketing improves over time instead of resetting with every vendor change.
- When my schedule has gaps, help fill them from past customers and referrals before I pay for cold traffic.

## 4. User roles

- Business owner: approves budgets, offers, claims, landing pages, and campaigns; reviews the monthly growth report; makes all spend decisions.
- Office manager: handles the leads the system produces through the normal intake process; flags quality problems.
- Nevamis founder (operator): designs and builds landing pages and experiments, configures campaigns and tracking, reviews everything before it goes live, runs the monthly growth review with the owner.
- The system: builds and serves conversion assets, routes and nurtures leads through the already-approved follow-up services, records attribution, and drafts reports and experiment recommendations. It does not set budgets, publish claims, or launch campaigns on its own.

## 5. Trigger

- Program triggers: a signed growth engagement with an approved budget, approved claims, and approved offers. Nothing runs before that approval exists in writing.
- Campaign triggers: a scheduled campaign start date approved by the owner; a landing page or experiment going live after founder review and owner sign-off.
- Journey triggers: a visitor lands on a tracked page, submits a campaign form, or calls a campaign-specific number; an existing lead reaches a nurture step; a reactivation or referral workflow condition is met under its own service's consent rules.
- Reporting triggers: a monthly growth review date, plus an optional weekly summary. Experiment results are reported when they reach their planned end date, not when they look good.

## 6. Inputs

- Approved inputs from the owner: budget per channel, offers, pricing claims, service-area limits, messaging boundaries, and brand assets. All claims must be true and approved; none are invented.
- Conversion assets: the existing website, new campaign landing pages, campaign-specific forms, and campaign-specific tracked phone numbers.
- Traffic and campaign data from the ad platforms in scope (spend, impressions, clicks) via API or monthly import.
- Lead, call, appointment, quote, job, and payment data from the underlying Nevamis services (AI Front Desk, Instant Lead Follow-Up, Automatic Lead Tracking, Revenue Clarity) and the client's job-management system.
- Consent and unsubscribe state for every contactable person, from the shared consent records used by the follow-up, reactivation, and referral services.
- Search content inputs: the client's real services, real service area, and real answers to customer questions. No fabricated reviews, case studies, or credentials.
- Written experiment plans: hypothesis, change, metric, sample or duration target, and stop rule, agreed before each experiment starts.

## 7. Decision rules

- Nothing customer-facing goes live without owner approval: pages, forms, ads, offers, claims, nurture sequences, and budgets are all approved first, then run within those approvals.
- Spend never exceeds the approved budget per channel per period. If a platform change or error risks overspend, campaigns pause first and questions are asked after.
- All outbound email and SMS follows CASL: recorded consent, sender identification, working unsubscribe, and honoured unsubscribe state shared across every Nevamis service. No purchased lists, no cold outreach to people without consent.
- Reactivation and referral contact follows the rules of those underlying services; the Growth System schedules and measures, it does not loosen anyone's contact rules. Frequency caps apply across the whole program so one person is never hammered by multiple workflows.
- Experiments change one thing at a time against a written plan, run to their planned end, and are judged on leads or collected revenue, never on vanity metrics alone.
- Attribution follows the written Revenue Clarity rules: collected revenue only, taxes and refunds excluded, written attribution window, unattributed revenue shown as unattributed, no guessing.
- No guarantees of lead volume or revenue, in any sales conversation, page, or report. Reports state what happened and what is attributable, nothing more.
- No performance-based pricing until attribution is reliable per the brief's checklist: sources identified, appointments linked, completed work reconciled, collected revenue verified, refunds accounted for, attribution rules written, and disputes resolvable from records.
- Retargeting audiences are built only from properly disclosed tracking, exclude sensitive categories, and are torn down when the engagement ends.

## 8. Actions

- Build and maintain conversion-focused landing pages matched to each campaign and service line, each with its own form and tracked phone number.
- Run website experiments: headline, offer, layout, and call-to-action tests against the written experiment plan, with results recorded whether they win or lose.
- Route every campaign lead into the existing instant follow-up and nurture workflows, using only approved templates and sequences.
- Produce search content for the client's real services and service area, drafted by the system, reviewed by the founder, approved by the owner before publishing.
- Support paid campaigns: build campaign structure, audiences, and creative drafts inside approved budgets and claims; monitor spend and pause anything that breaches limits.
- Configure retargeting for site visitors who did not convert, within the consent and disclosure rules above.
- Schedule reactivation and referral pushes through the underlying services when the owner wants to fill capacity.
- Record every touch into the attribution layer and reconcile campaign spend against collected revenue each month.
- Draft the monthly growth report and a ranked list of proposed next experiments for the owner to approve or reject.

Mixed AI action levels: drafting, recommending, and reporting are Level 1; running approved sequences and pausing overspending campaigns are Level 2 within fixed limits. Publishing pages, launching campaigns, changing budgets, and approving claims always remain human decisions.

## 9. Outputs

- Live, tracked landing pages and campaign forms owned by the client.
- A monthly growth report: spend, leads, qualified leads, appointments, jobs, collected attributable revenue per channel, and the headline ratio of collected attributable revenue to total program cost (fees plus ad spend plus usage).
- Experiment log: every test run, its plan, its result, and what was kept.
- Campaign summaries with plain-language explanations, not platform jargon.
- A proposed next-month plan (experiments, budget allocation, content) that the owner approves, edits, or rejects.
- An exceptions list: overspend near-misses, paused campaigns, complaint or unsubscribe spikes, and data gaps, stated plainly.

## 10. Human approval points

- The owner approves the initial program: budgets, channels, offers, claims, and service-area scope, in writing, before anything runs.
- The owner approves every landing page, ad, and nurture sequence before it goes live, and re-approves when claims or offers change.
- The founder reviews every page, campaign, and report before the owner sees it during the pilot phase.
- Budget changes, new channels, and new offers each require fresh owner approval; approval of one campaign never generalizes to the next.
- Experiment plans are approved before starting; experiments are not extended or redefined mid-run without a documented decision.
- Any move toward a performance-pricing component requires the attribution reliability checklist to pass and a separate written agreement.

## 11. Failure states

- A campaign overspends because of a platform error, misconfiguration, or delayed spend reporting.
- Landing pages break, load slowly, or lose tracking, so paid traffic converts poorly or converts invisibly.
- Lead quality drops: a campaign produces volume but the leads do not book or are outside the service area.
- A claim or offer runs that the owner did not approve, or an approved offer expires but keeps running.
- Follow-up misfires: duplicate messages, contact after unsubscribe, or frequency caps breached across overlapping workflows.
- Attribution breaks (integration failure, untracked numbers), making the headline revenue-to-cost ratio unreliable.
- The program costs more than it returns for a sustained period and this is not surfaced quickly.
- An ad account is suspended or a platform policy change halts delivery.
- Search content drifts into unverifiable claims, or an experiment is quietly judged on a vanity metric.

## 12. Fallback

- Any campaign can be paused immediately, by the founder or the owner, without breaking the rest of the stack; capture and follow-up keep running on organic traffic.
- If spend tracking is delayed or unreliable, campaigns pause at the approved budget cap by default rather than trusting the platform to stop.
- If a landing page fails, traffic is redirected to the main site's closest working page and the incident is logged in the monthly report.
- If attribution becomes unreliable, reporting downgrades honestly: affected figures are marked incomplete, no numbers are estimated, and any performance discussions stop until attribution is repaired.
- If a claim or messaging error reaches the public, the asset is corrected or pulled the same day, the owner is told, and the correction is documented.
- If the program is not covering its cost, the monthly review says so plainly and recommends reducing scope or stopping; the system never buries a losing month.
- The client can wind down the program and keep their landing pages, content, ad accounts, and historical reports.

## 13. Data stored

- Approved program records: budgets, claims, offers, approvals with dates, and every version of customer-facing assets.
- Campaign and spend records per channel, and page analytics (visits, conversions) collected with privacy-conscious tooling.
- Lead and journey records linking source, campaign, page, conversation, appointment, quote, job, and collected payment, shared with the attribution layer.
- Consent, source, purpose, and unsubscribe state for every contactable person, as required by CASL.
- Experiment plans, results, and decisions.
- Issued reports and the record set behind each figure, plus an audit log of material actions (launches, pauses, budget changes, corrections).

## 14. Data not stored

- Payment card numbers, banking credentials, or any payment credentials (payment references and amounts only, from connected systems).
- Ad platform, CRM, or email passwords; access is via least-privilege API connections and delegated account access, never stored client passwords.
- Sensitive personal information about end customers (health details, government IDs, financial details) in analytics, audiences, or lead records.
- Retargeting audiences built on sensitive categories or on data collected without disclosure.
- Purchased contact lists, scraped contacts, or any contact record without a recorded consent basis.
- Data from business lines, locations, or accounts outside the agreed scope.

## 15. Retention

- Journey, campaign, and reconciliation records: retained for the engagement plus 24 months, matching the Revenue Clarity retention rule, because they support issued reports and any future performance billing, then deleted.
- Consent and unsubscribe records: retained as long as required to honour and evidence them.
- Leads that never become customers: personal identifiers minimized after 12 months, kept only in aggregate counts.
- Experiment logs and issued reports: retained as business records for the same period.
- On termination: the client receives their pages, content, report archive, and attribution export; retargeting audiences are deleted; identifiable records are deleted on the agreed schedule, subject to legal and accounting retention obligations.
- Retention terms are written into the service agreement, consistent with Canadian and Alberta privacy requirements (PIPEDA and PIPA).

## 16. Integration requirements

- The underlying Nevamis stack, live and stable for this client: AI Front Desk (Twilio, ElevenLabs, Cal.com), Instant Lead Follow-Up, Automatic Lead Tracking, and Revenue Clarity. This service depends on them and is not offered without them.
- Web: hosting and deployment for landing pages consistent with the client's site, form handling, and privacy-conscious analytics.
- Telephony: Twilio campaign-specific tracked numbers per channel.
- Ad platforms as approved per client: Google Ads and Meta first; one or two platforms per client during the pilot.
- Email and SMS: the client's existing approved sending setup from the follow-up services, including shared unsubscribe state.
- CRM or job management: the client's system (Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, or similar) for job and payment outcomes.
- Automation layer: n8n, Make, Zapier, or direct APIs for event movement.
- For each integration, document API availability, authentication, permissions, webhooks, rate limits, failure behavior, and data accessed, per the integration roadmap. Do not state publicly that an integration exists until it is implemented and tested.

## 17. Test cases

1. End-to-end campaign journey: a click on a test Google Ads campaign lands on the campaign page, the form is submitted, instant follow-up fires within the agreed window, a booking is made, and after job completion the collected amount (excluding GST) appears against that campaign in the monthly report.
2. Campaign number attribution: a call to the campaign-specific tracked number is answered by the AI Front Desk, booked, completed, and paid; the report attributes the collected revenue to that campaign, not to the general line.
3. Budget cap enforcement: a test campaign with a $500 monthly cap approaches the cap; the campaign pauses at or before the cap, the pause is logged, and the owner is notified. No overspend occurs.
4. Unapproved claim blocked: a drafted ad containing an offer the owner has not approved cannot go live; it is held in the review queue and appears on the founder's approval list instead of publishing.
5. Unsubscribe honoured across the program: a person who unsubscribes from a nurture email receives no further email or SMS from any workflow in the program (nurture, reactivation, referral), verified by attempting each trigger against the test contact.
6. Experiment discipline: an A/B headline test with a written plan runs to its planned end even though the variant leads early; the result is logged with its plan, and the losing variant's data is kept in the experiment log.
7. Broken tracking degradation: the tracking number on one landing page is misconfigured for a week; the monthly report marks that channel's figures incomplete for those dates, and no numbers are estimated to fill the gap.
8. Losing month reported honestly: a test month where total program cost exceeds collected attributable revenue produces a report that states the negative ratio plainly and recommends corrective action, with no reframing to vanity metrics.

## 18. Pilot limits

- 1 to 2 pilot clients, both established Nevamis clients already running the Front Desk, follow-up, lead tracking, and Revenue Clarity with at least two months of clean attribution data.
- One or two paid channels per client, modest owner-approved budgets, one business location and one business line.
- Landing pages and experiments limited to an agreed monthly volume the founder can review properly (suggested: up to 3 new pages and 2 concurrent experiments per client).
- Founder reviews every page, campaign, sequence, and report before it reaches the public or the owner.
- Retainer pricing only. No performance-based pricing during the pilot, per the brief: performance pricing is not sold until attribution is reliable.
- No public claims about pilot results, no case studies, and no client names used in marketing without separate written permission and verified numbers.
- Keep the service listed in the Future stage publicly until the pilot proves it can be delivered consistently.

## 19. Success metrics

- Primary, from the brief: collected attributable revenue relative to total program cost (Nevamis fees plus ad spend plus usage). Target: the ratio is positive and improving by month three of a pilot, with the unattributed share shown honestly.
- More qualified opportunities: qualified leads per month above the client's pre-program baseline.
- Better conversion: landing page conversion rate (visit to call, form, or booking) improving against the first-month baseline.
- Stronger retention of demand: reactivation and referral workflows contribute measurable booked jobs.
- Clearer return on acquisition spending: the owner can state cost and collected revenue per channel at the monthly review, and makes at least one documented budget decision using the data.
- Zero compliance incidents: no CASL complaints, no unapproved claims published, no budget overruns.
- Pilot clients continue and expand after the pilot period.

## 20. Commercial assumptions

All figures are internal hypothesis, not approved, in Canadian dollars, taken from the brief's illustrative internal ranges. They are not public prices.

- Setup: $2,500 to $10,000 (landing pages, tracking, campaign builds, experiment framework, program rules workshop).
- Monthly: $1,500 to $5,000 plus advertising and usage (management fee; ad spend is billed at cost through the client's accounts, and messaging or AI usage is passed through transparently).
- Optional performance component, future only: the brief's starting hypothesis is a base retainer plus approximately 5 to 10 percent (preferred 7.5 percent) of collected, attributable net-new revenue, with a limited attribution window and an optional monthly cap during the first test. Not offered until the attribution reliability checklist passes, and never advertised as a profit-share headline.
- Pricing must model software cost, AI usage, telephony and messaging usage, founder onboarding time, ongoing support, failure recovery, reporting time, refund risk, payment-processing fees, gross margin, and client value; do not price by marking up software or ad spend.
- Positioned as the top of the Nevamis Grow package, after the Review and Referral Engine and Revenue Clarity. Modular adoption applies to the rest of the stack, but this service has hard prerequisites and is the highest-touch, highest-price offering.

## 21. Support requirements

- Founder-led onboarding: program rules workshop (budgets, claims, offers, boundaries), page and campaign builds, and tracking verification (estimate 15 to 25 hours per client at pilot stage; this is the heaviest onboarding in the stack).
- Ongoing: weekly campaign and spend check, experiment reviews, and a monthly growth review meeting with the owner (estimate 6 to 10 hours per client per month during the pilot).
- Spend and delivery monitoring with alerts; a campaign spending anomaly is acknowledged the same business day.
- A written incident process for public-facing errors (wrong claim, broken page, messaging misfire): correct or pull the asset, notify the owner, document the correction.
- A written dispute process for attributed figures, shared with Revenue Clarity: the founder re-checks challenged numbers against stored records and documents the outcome.
- Client-facing documentation: what is approved and where, how leads are handled, how attribution works, and what the monthly report means.
- Support channel: email and phone to the founder during the pilot; no self-serve support tier yet.

- [ ] Prerequisite services (Front Desk, Instant Lead Follow-Up, Automatic Lead Tracking, Revenue Clarity) live and stable for the pilot client, with at least two months of clean attribution data.
- [ ] Program approval pack template written: budgets, channels, offers, claims, service area, and messaging boundaries, all owner-signed before launch.
- [ ] Landing page template built on the client's stack, tested for speed, mobile, accessibility, and tracking, with campaign form and tracked number verified end to end.
- [ ] Budget caps and pause behavior tested on each ad platform in scope, including the overspend near-miss case.
- [ ] Approval queue enforced: no page, ad, or sequence can reach the public without recorded owner approval.
- [ ] CASL controls verified across the whole program: consent records, sender identification, working unsubscribe, shared unsubscribe state, and cross-workflow frequency caps.
- [ ] Experiment plan template and experiment log in place; one full test-to-decision cycle run on internal or demo assets.
- [ ] Monthly growth report template approved: collected revenue only, cost-to-revenue ratio as the headline, unattributed share shown, no guarantees or causation language.
- [ ] All eight test cases in section 17 passing on pilot or sandbox data.
- [ ] Privacy review done: data minimized, retention and deletion defined, least-privilege access, processor agreements in place, audit logs on, retargeting disclosure in place.
- [ ] Risk register entries added for excessive follow-up, contacting ineligible recipients, revenue misattribution, incorrect pricing claims, and vendor or ad-platform outage.
- [ ] Owner walkthrough completed: what the system does alone, what always requires approval, and how to pause everything.
